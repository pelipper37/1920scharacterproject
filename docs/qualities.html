Qualities

Virtues

  • Imagination : The most innovative technologies and theories come with a lot of imagination. It's almost a prerequisite.
  • Creativity : Mr. Horaz has shown an ability to offer unconventional strategies, and although they are not always effective, some of them are definitely more effective than conventional tactics.

  • Education : Mr. Horaz has an excellent education, graduating from Harvard at the age of 20.
  • Knowledge : Education doesn't always translate into knowledge, but Mr. Horaz has shown his knowledge in mathematics, economics, and philosophy. These would almost definitely be useful in the future.

  • Influence : Mr. Horaz has the ability to influence other people. If he wants something done, it will likely get done. If he wants a program stopped, it will probably be halted. It is something we look for in an operative.
  • Authority : Having influence doesn't always mean it always works, but Mr. Horaz does have a great amount of authority so that what he wants gets done more often than not. It is a quality worth looking into, if not now, then in the future.
  • Self-Control : Having power and then abusing it creates enemies, but abuse of power is not something that appears to be a vice of Mr. Horaz. This may be difficult to exploit.

  • Enigma : No one knows much about what he does outside of his work. This could be a virtue or it could be a vice. More on that later.
